How this site is built

Our data, in plain
view.

Whoadunit doesn't claim to be perfect. It claims to be transparent. Every name, party affiliation, and election result here came from a public source. Below: which sources, what's missing, and how to challenge anything you spot.

Four things we promise

Sourced, sourced,
sourced.

Four commitments, in priority order. Number one matters more than the rest combined.

Principle 01
Sourced, always.

Every record points back to a published page. If we can't cite it, we don't publish it.

02 · Politically neutral
Same treatment, every party.

A PAS ADUN from Kelantan and a DAP MP from Penang get the same completeness score, the same wording, the same place on the page.

03 · Open licence All data here is published under CC BY-SA 4.0 . Reuse it. Build on it.
04 · Honestly incomplete
A 60% profile says 60%.

We surface missing fields rather than hide them. Honest gaps are more useful than a false impression of completeness.

What we don't have

The list of honest gaps.

If you find something stale here, it almost certainly falls under one of these. Spot one, and the volunteer page can fix it.

  • 1 By-elections. Vacancies filled between general or state elections aren't yet reflected for every seat.
  • 2 Defections and party changes mid-term. A representative who switched parties since the last election may still appear under their original party.
  • 3 Resignations and disqualifications. Seats vacated for legal or personal reasons may not have a replacement listed yet.
  • 4 Service-centre contact details. Whose parties don't publish a service centre, or whose centre changed location, often have phone or office gaps.
