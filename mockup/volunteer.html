How to help

Four ways to contribute

You don't need to be a developer or a political science grad. You just need to know where to look — and a few minutes.

1
Find the ADUN's contact from their party website

Most parties (PKR, DAP, PAS, UMNO etc.) list service centre details on their website. Search "[ADUN name] pejabat khidmat" or "[party] [seat name]" and you'll often find an address or phone.

2
Check their Facebook or Instagram page

Many ADUNs have active social media pages with their office address in the bio, or a contact number pinned as a post. Screenshots help — include the URL when you submit.

3
Submit a correction or addition below

Fill in the form below with the ADUN's name, their seat, and the contact detail you found. Include a source link so our team can verify it before publishing.

4
If you're technical — join us on GitHub

The dataset is structured JSON. Pull requests with sourced data are welcome. See the data schema and contribution guide in the repository README.

Our principles

Open data,
always

Whoadunit is built on a few principles we won't compromise on.

🔓
Always free to access
No paywall. No login required to find your representative. The data belongs to the public.
✅
Every change is sourced
We don't publish unverified data. Every contact detail has a source link on record, even if we don't show it publicly.
⚖️
Politically neutral
We cover all parties equally. A PAS ADUN from Kelantan gets the same completeness score and treatment as a DAP MP from Penang.
📊
Transparent about gaps
We'd rather show a 20% completeness score than hide the missing fields. Honest incompleteness is more useful than a false impression of completeness.
